Inigo Iribarren Aguirre


Iñigo carried out his chemistry studies at “Universidad Autónoma de Madrid” as well as his master studies in theoretical chemistry and computational modelling (TCCM).
He spent three months at “Sorbonne Université” in Paris under the supervision of Dr. Julia Contreras developing a module for the Non-covalent interaction index software that separates an interaction into orbitalic contributions.

He carried out his master thesis at “Instituto de Química Médica (CSIC, Madrid)” under the supervision of Prof. Ibon Alkorta.
This master thesis was focused on studying dimers formed by two protonated monomers linked by hydrogen bonds and characterize those interactions (Phys. Chem. Chem. Phys., 2019,21, 5796-5802)”.

Currently, Iñigo is at Trinity College Dublin doing his PhD under the supervision of Prof. Cristina Trujillo focused on the computational design of novel asymmetric organocatalysts.
He has also performed different side projects that resulted in several publications.

Along with his research activity he has been involved in more than 20 outreach activities all over Ireland with three different programs like Stories from the lab, Spectroscopy in a suitcase and Energy investigators in which he and other PhD students go to different high schools engaging young students and showing them how chemistry looks at university and all the different fields that form it.

During this time he has also presented his work in more than 10 symposiums and conferences and he was awarded with the best flash talk award at the ICI postgraduate day in 2020.
